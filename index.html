Shadow over Ragusa

The first part of an epic historical saga where love blooms amid the shadows of war and a hidden conspiracy threatens the fate of a proud maritime republic.

About the Book

Shadow over Ragusa is the opening novel of a historical saga set in the Republic of Ragusa, where diplomacy, trade, faith, and ambition collide beneath the shadow of war.

Blending mystery, romance, and political intrigue, the novel follows characters drawn into a dangerous conspiracy that threatens the fragile balance of a small but powerful maritime republic.

This first installment begins a trilogy that continues in Republic in the Mist and concludes in Sunset of Liberty.

Synopsis

Centuries ago in the Mediterranean Adriatic Sea, the Republic of Ragusa reached its peak. Independence was maintained through diplomacy, trade, and patriotism. As Venetian pressure grew in their ambition to conquer Ragusa once again, so too did the fleet of a new type of warship being built across Italian ports. Villa Marinowitch, residence of respected noble council member Lord Ivano, often hosted gatherings of his peers and their families to wine and dine. On one such occasion in the lord’s library, several members of the council, along with the Archbishop of Ragusa, held a discussion on current political affairs. Once it was over, Master of Keys Luka, former guardian of Ragusa, found a scroll dropped on the floor. Not by his own intent, he and his friend Iva Lukich were drawn into a web of secrets. Following the mysterious scroll in search of a clue, a trip to Lokrum Island reveals a hidden launch, a golden chained rosary, and a naked body concealed beneath broken boards. The troubling discovery soon unravels into a deeper conspiracy. From the ruined monastery to the very heart of the council chamber, Ragusa’s political stability begins to shift under renewed Venetian pressure. With increased shipments of goods, the Church begins to speak in favour of the Venetian states, while Lord Ivano grows doubtful. The Admiralty counts ships and cannons, and the Council seeks balance under Duke Orlando. Yet beneath garden parties and lantern-lit dinners, tensions begin to rise. Father Marko, Archbishop of the Holy Church council, carries hidden intent. Messages from beyond the sea whisper orders to spies. Rumours of red-cloaked figures circulate within the city walls, shrouded in mystery. The Pope himself, along with his ally, the Duke of Venice, prepares for invasion, while Luka’s patience and Iva’s courage lead them through markets, harbours, and shadowed paths as they uncover connections between clergy, foreign agents, and unseen forces. Into this gathering storm sails Miho Lupina, a young officer whose sense of duty mirrors the city’s wisdom and strength. His presence brings both hope and resolve, as growing affection binds his fate to that of Iva Lukich from the moment they first meet. Suspicion deepens as danger closes in. Luka and Iva find themselves at the centre of a struggle where loyalty, truth, and cunning may determine the future of a republic standing on the edge of uncertainty. Shadow over Ragusa blends mystery, romance, and political intrigue into the story of a small maritime republic caught between empires. Miho and Iva’s love grows strong and leads them, each in their own way, to the Sultan’s court, where an impossible choice awaits them. This is the first title in a historical saga in which fictional characters live through events, some of which truly occurred in the republic that once stood proudly. The story continues in Republic in the Mist and concludes in Sunset of Liberty.

Author Bio

Mijo Radalj is the author of Shadow over Ragusa, a historical saga inspired by the heritage, atmosphere, and political complexity of the Republic of Ragusa.

His work blends fictional characters with historically inspired events, bringing to life a world of diplomacy, maritime power, intrigue, and sacrifice. Through rich settings and emotionally driven storytelling, he explores the fate of a republic once positioned between great empires.

Shadow over Ragusa is the first novel in the trilogy, followed by Republic in the Mist and Sunset of Liberty.
