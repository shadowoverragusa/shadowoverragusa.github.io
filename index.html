Shadow over Ragusa

A historical novel set in the Republic of Ragusa, where pure love endures all trials and tribulations.

Synopsis

Cover art

Centuries ago on the Adriatic, the Republic of Ragusa survives through diplomacy, trade, and the vigilance of its walls. As Venetian pressure grows from the west, a quieter danger begins to move within the city itself. Inside Villa Marinowhitch, Master of Keys Luka and the capable steward Iva are drawn into a web of secrets after a clandestine trip to Lokrum Island reveals a hidden launch, a golden chain, and a body concealed beneath broken boards. What begins as a troubling discovery becomes the first thread of a conspiracy reaching from monastery corridors to the council chamber. Ragusa’s political life moves through ritual and ceremony. The Church speaks, the Admiralty counts ships and cannons, and the Council seeks balance under Duke Orlando. Yet beneath garden gatherings and lantern lit dinners, loyalties begin to fracture. Father Marko’s counsel carries its own shadows, letters from powerful men beyond the sea urge dangerous alliances, and whispers of red cloaked figures spread along the walls. Luka’s patience and Iva’s clear eyed resolve lead them through markets, harbors, and hidden chapels as they uncover ties between churchmen, foreign agents, and smugglers working the coves around Lokrum. Into this tightening storm sails Miho Lupina, a young officer whose sense of duty mirrors the city’s own. His arrival brings both hope and risk and binds private feeling to civic responsibility. As suspicion deepens and Ragusa’s future grows uncertain, the paths of Luka, Iva, and Miho converge in a struggle where loyalty, faith, and love may decide the fate of a fragile republic. Shadow over Ragusa blends mystery, romance, and political intrigue into the story of a small city standing between empires and of the people whose quiet courage keeps its freedom alive.

About the Author

Mijo Radalj (born October 29, 1986) is a Croatian writer whose path to storytelling began during seasonal work as a tourist bus driver along the Adriatic coast. Listening to local guides tell versions of history that never reached the official books, shaped by memory, rumor, and place, sparked his fascination with untold stories. Before moving to Ireland, those seasons gave him a front row seat to temperaments and relationships that later inspired characters. What started as a light, just for fun idea grew into a full historical novel. Shadow over Ragusa is the result: a work born of lived experience and the spaces between history and myth. The world of the novel draws on Dubrovnik’s civic spirit and the private loyalties of families like the Marinowhitch, where service, restraint, and courage quietly protect a small republic between empires.
